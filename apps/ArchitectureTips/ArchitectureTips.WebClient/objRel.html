Association

  • Association shows the structural relationship between objects
  • Association has a multiplicity: (1..1), (1..*), (*..*)
  • Association may have a direction: unidirectional, bidirectional

Composition

  • A musician knows his instruments
  • Instruments "do not know" anything about its musicians
  • Instruments cannot exist without a musician

Aggregation

  • The group "knows" about its musicians
  • Musicians may "know" your group
  • Musicians can exist without any group

The proper inheritance

  • Class does not promise more, but at least provides a functional minimum
  • Class can be polymorphic, but no more
  • The type can be interchangeable in meaning
Incorrect inheritance

What causes incorrect inheritance

  • The appearance of downcasts
  • The appearance of procedural if-else-if, switch-case with type checking
  • Brings practical benefits of polymorphism to the zero

Inclusion/Delegation

Heterogeneous data structures (generic)

  • Covariance - If the T-type can be transformed into the R-type it follows that IOperation<T> can be safely transformed into IOperation<R>
  • Contravariant - If the R-type can be transformed into the T-type it follows that IOperation<T> can be safely transformed into IOperation<R>
  • Invariance - For R- and T- types there is no conversion
